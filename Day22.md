Day 22/60 — #60DayClaudeChallenge
Project: SkillBridge AI — AI-powered career platform for students & fresh graduates
Assumption Tested Today
I ran a full startup validation analysis for SkillBridge AI using Claude, and one core assumption got challenged:

Assumption going in: Since the problem is clearly validated (82% of 500+ surveyed students can't identify which skills employers actually want), the freemium → paid conversion would be the "easy part."

What the analysis surfaced instead:

In price-sensitive markets like Tier-2/Tier-3 India, willingness-to-pay is the actual unknown — not the problem itself. Strong signals like 150 waitlist signups and a 4.5/5 prototype satisfaction score validate that the pain is real and the solution resonates, but they say nothing about who will convert to paid.
Reframe
Instead of defaulting to B2C freemium as the primary revenue engine, the analysis pushed toward testing a B2B-first model: licensing directly to universities already incentivized to improve placement outcomes, with student access bundled in. This reduces consumer CAC risk and uses institutional trust as the distribution channel.
Why This Matters
It's a good reminder that "the problem exists" and "the business model is proven" are two completely separate claims — and conflating them is an easy trap in early validation.
